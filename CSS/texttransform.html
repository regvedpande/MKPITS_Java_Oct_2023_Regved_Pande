Nuclear Physics

Nuclear physics is a branch of physics that studies the properties and behavior of atomic nuclei. It explores the structure of atomic nuclei, their interactions, and the processes involving nuclear reactions. This field encompasses topics like nuclear decay, fission, fusion, and the behavior of subatomic particles within atomic nuclei. Nuclear physics plays a crucial role in understanding fundamental aspects of matter, energy, and the universe, with applications ranging from energy production in nuclear reactors to medical diagnostics and treatments.
