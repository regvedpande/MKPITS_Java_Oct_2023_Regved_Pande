Vedic Age

The Vedic Age, spanning from around 1500 BCE to 600 BCE, witnessed the composition of the sacred Vedas, forming the cornerstone of Hinduism. Society evolved from pastoral and nomadic to settled communities, establishing a four-fold social structure and the foundation of the caste system. Vedic religion was characterized by polytheistic rituals and sacrifices dedicated to various deities. Political organization transitioned from tribal kingships to territorial states known as janapadas. The Vedic Age laid the groundwork for later philosophical developments seen in texts like the Upanishads and Brahmanas.
