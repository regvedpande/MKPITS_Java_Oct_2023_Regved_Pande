Meteors

Meteors, also known as shooting stars, are celestial objects that enter Earth's atmosphere at high speeds, creating a spectacular streak of light as they burn up due to friction. These cosmic travelers are often remnants of asteroids or comets, composed of various minerals and metals. The fleeting brilliance of meteors is a result of intense heat generated during their atmospheric entry. Meteor showers occur when Earth passes through the debris trail left by a comet, leading to a heightened display of these luminous phenomena. Studying meteors provides valuable insights into the composition and origins of our solar system.
